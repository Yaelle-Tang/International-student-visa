VISA Shutdown Something Blabla

By Yaelle, Yusuf, Raginee

After a three-week pause in student visa interviews this summer and newly expanded travel bans under the Trump administration, international student arrivals to the U.S. fell by 19% this August. It was the steepest non-pandemic decline on record. We want to uncover what is truly behind this drop, what the underlying story is that people are experiencing, and how these political decisions are impacting students’ lives. For instance, there are many Chinese students in the U.S. who are talking about this on personal Chinese podcasts. They worry about being forced to leave the country or being denied entry, especially those in sensitive STEM fields.

We will plan to combine government data on visa delays and student arrivals with personal stories from students who’ve been stuck, delayed, or forced to change their plans. Likewise, it will address how the drop in international student enrollment is affecting university funding and how countries in the EU and China are wooing talented students rejected by America.

The New York Times has already mapped the decline, but its story stops at the national level. This story will zoom in on the human and local dimensions, using both visuals and personal storytelling to explore how federal policy ripples through university campuses.
